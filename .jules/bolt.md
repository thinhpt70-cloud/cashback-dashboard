## 2024-05-18 - React.memo Optimization Pitfalls
**Learning:** Adding `React.memo` blindly to complex components that receive unstable object or function references (e.g. `currencyFn` inline arrow functions, un-memoized derived arrays like `data`) is an anti-pattern. While it won't necessarily break functionality if `React` is properly imported, it fails to provide the intended performance benefit because the shallow prop comparison will always fail, causing re-renders anyway and adding the overhead of the comparison itself.
**Action:** Before applying `React.memo`, thoroughly trace all props passed to the component up to their origin to ensure they are properly stable (memoized via `useMemo` or `useCallback`). If ensuring stability is too complex or architectural, prefer optimizing inner bottlenecks (like expensive map/filter operations) rather than memoizing the whole component. Always verify that `import React` or `import { memo }` is present to avoid `ReferenceError`.
